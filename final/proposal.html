Final Project Proposal

Student: Aloe Pereira

1. The title and topic of my site

Title: Aloe's Anime Hub
Topic: A website that highlights popular anime, beginner recommendations, character spotlights, anime genres, and anime fan culture.

2. Why I chose this topic

I chose anime as my topic because it has been one of my favorite hobbies for many years. Anime inspires creativity and teaches powerful lessons about courage, friendship, sacrifice, and growth. I enjoy the art styles, the stories, and the emotions that anime can express. By creating this site, I want to share helpful recommendations and explain anime in a positive, uplifting way. I will make sure all content is appropriate and follows the Honor Code.

3. Content on the home page and each of the five pages

Home Page

  • Welcome message introducing “Aloe's Anime Hub”
  • Short explanation of what the site is about
  • Featured anime section with a highlight image and brief description
  • Navigation links to all other pages

Page 1: Top Anime Recommendations

  • List of beginner-friendly and popular anime series
  • Short summary for each anime
  • Poster-style images (using copyright-safe sources)
  • “Best for” tags such as Action, Fantasy, Slice of Life, etc.

Page 2: Character Spotlights (Table Page)

  • Profiles of several iconic anime characters
  • Images of the characters (using safe, allowed images)
  • Short bios, personality traits, and what makes them memorable
  • A table comparing characters, with columns such as:
    • Character Name
    • Anime Title
    • Role (Main / Side)
    • Power Type or Ability
    • Notable Personality Trait

Page 3: Anime Genres Explained

  • Definitions of major genres (for example: shounen, shoujo, seinen, isekai, slice of life)
  • Example anime for each genre
  • Simple text or icons to visually separate each genre section

Page 4: Anime Art & Fandom Culture

  • Explanation of anime art styles (chibi, realistic, stylized, etc.)
  • Short section about cosplay, conventions, and online fan communities
  • Reminder about choosing media that is clean and Honor Code appropriate
  • Royalty-free or permission-based images related to art tools, drawing, or fandom scenes

Page 5: Contact / Feedback Form

  • Short message inviting visitors to share their thoughts
  • A form where users can enter their information and feedback

4. Form fields and table data

Form Fields (on the Contact / Feedback page)

  • Full Name (text box)
  • Email Address (email field)
  • Your Favorite Anime (text box)
  • Your Favorite Genre (dropdown - e.g., Action, Romance, Comedy, Slice of Life, etc.)
  • How long you have been watching anime (dropdown or text box)
  • Comment or Feedback (multi-line text area)

Table Data (on the Character Spotlights page)

The table will compare different anime characters. Each row will be one character, with:

  • Character Name
  • Anime Title
  • Role (Main / Side)
  • Power Type or Ability
  • Notable Personality Trait

Example row: Tanjiro Kamado - Demon Slayer - Main - Water Breathing - Kind and determined.

5. How I will collect information, images, and content (with references)

I will collect information by using trusted anime databases and official sources for basic facts, such as summaries, release years, and character names. I will also use my own knowledge and experience watching anime to describe why I recommend certain shows. For images, I will use copyright-free resources (public domain or Creative Commons) and make sure I follow the rules for using each image. I will not upload copyrighted screenshots or fan art without permission.

Some references I plan to use include:

  • MyAnimeList - general anime information and summaries
  • Anime News Network - background details on anime and staff
  • Wikimedia Commons - public domain or Creative Commons images
  • Crunchyroll News / Blogs - general genre and culture explanations
  • Other official anime websites or clean review sites as needed
